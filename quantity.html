Fuel Quantity issues ?

It's quite normal to doubt the fuel quantity dispensed by the dispensing machine. Let us understand the If's and But's of this issue

Sometimes when you visit your favourite gas station, you either ask to topup the tank in Liters or with amount (for example Rs. 500/-). However both methods differ. After refuelling you tend to check your vehicle fuel guage, and then you find yourself in a question, have they delivered it correctly ?
When you ask to deliver in volume, the dispensing machine calculate the amount accordingly and delivers the volume. If you ask to refill in amount, then the dispensing machine calculates the volume accordingly (Now this volume depends on fuel rates, if the rate is high you get less volume)

If you are in any doubts, and if you want to check the fuel quantity dispensed by the dispenser is correct or not, Just ask the Gas Station manager to give a 5L measure test. A 5L measure test is a conical measure provided by the govt (with certificate of caliberation) to check the accuracy of the dispenser. the accuracy of the dispensed fuel should be in the range of Plus or Minus 2ml per Liter.

Is my vehicle guage accurate ?

No. Vehicle guages(digital or analog) is just an approximation of the volume present in the tank. This approximation accuracy may vary based on the manufacturer.
In real life example, if your vehicle is parked up the slope your guage may increase, and down the slope it may decrease.
In case of analog fuel guages, the device present inside the tank may vary with the age.
However in case of digital fuel guages, where guage representation is in-terms of bars. Suppose a vehicle manufacturer claims a 35Litre tank, and each digital bar represent 7L. then 7(litre) x 5(bar) = 35L(total)
What if your tank contains 10L fuel, should it show you 1 Bar or 2 Bar (this is a big confusing question because digital guages does not have an intermediate step unlike analog guages). Hence the best way to check the quantity of fuel is 5L measure test.
