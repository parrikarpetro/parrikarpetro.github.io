Fuelled more than the tank capacity ?

When you see that the delivered quantity is more than the tank capacity mentioned in the vehicle manual book. It's quite normal to doubt the fuel quantity dispensed by the dispensing machine.

At a Gas Station when you ask to make your tank full, the service boy makes your tank full up to the tank lead cover and then you start doubting the Petrol Pump station. Let us explain you with an example.
When a vehicle manufacturer say the tank capacity is 35L(say) they mean that this tank can hold 35L fuel "safely".
As per the government ARAI rules, every tank should have a minimum safe holding capacity and there should be an expansion buffer (an exta space in the tank in case fuel expands due to rise in temperature).

Hence the following three conditions determine the vehicle tank capacity:

  • Minimum Safe capacity: This is the official tank capacity given in every vehicle manual book
  • Buffer Capacity: Fuel expansion buffer, in order to withstand any expansion of fuel occured due to rise in temperature. This capacity is not mentioned in manual book
  • Tank & Hose pipe: A tank is connected to the outer body opening with a hose pipe, capacity of this is not mentioned in the manual book

Hence the above mentioned three component adds to the total vehicle fuel holding capacity. As per our analysis, every vehicle can hold 10-15% extra fuel.(considering the tank is near empty)

Safety suggestions from us

It is always recommended not to make the tank completely full. for example, if the tank is completely full up to the cover cap, and you park your vehicle on the slope. Then there are high chances that fuel might leak thus posing the risk.

What is Nozzle Auto Stop ?

Nozzle Auto Stop is a safety feature in place in order to prevent overflow in case the tank is full. When fuel touches the sensor of nozzles it auto shuts. Sometimes when the nozzle spout is placed inside the tank touches the inner wall. The sensor may trigger and shut the nozzle. It gives you an impression that the tank is full, however this may occur because the nozzle is wrongly placed or because of the narrow hose pipe connecting the tank.
